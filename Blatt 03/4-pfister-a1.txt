Hungrarian notation is an identifier naming convention
 in programming where a variable name starts with a 
 group of lower-case letters (indicate type), followed 
 by whatever name the programmer has chosen.

Examples:

bBusy : boolean
cApples : count of items
fBusy : float (or flag)
